Country Sourdough

Description

Developed by Chad Robertson in Northern California in the early 2000s, this country sourdough revolutionized artisan bread baking.

This bread boasts a shattery, crispy crust, and a moist, airy crumb. Available at a Tartine Bakery near you.

Ingredients

  • 100 grams leaven
  • 450 grams white-bread flour
  • 50 grams whole-wheat flour
  • 375 grams warm water
  • 10 grams fine sea salt

Steps

  1. Make leaven.
  2. Make dough by mixing leaven with the flours and 350 grams of water.
  3. After the autolyse period, mix in sea salt and remaining water.
  4. Let dough bulk ferment. Complete 3-4 sessions of folds in 30-minute increments.
  5. Remove from bowl, preshape into round, and let rest.
  6. Shape into final batard shape and place upside down in banneton.
  7. Let dough rise for 3-4 hours.
  8. At 3-hour mark, preheat oven to 500 degrees.
  9. Flip dough into dutch oven, score the top, and close dutch oven lid. Bake for 20 minutes.
  10. Remove the lid and bake for another 30 minutes.
  11. Remove from oven and place bread on cooling rack until fully cooled.
