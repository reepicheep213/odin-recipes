Blueberry Ricotta Pancakes

Description

Airy and moist, these ricotta pancakes with blueberries were a creation of Chef Neal Fraser and served at his restaurant BLD. The dish was featured on Food Network's The Best Thing I Ever Ate.

Standing for "breakfast, lunch, and dinner," BLD was a popular restaurant located on Beverly Blvd in Los Angeles. It remained popular until Fraser decided to close its doors for good in 2017.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 1 3/4 cups plus 2 tablespoons milk
  • 6 ounces ricotta cheese (1/2 cup plus 2 tablespoons)1
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • Unsalted butter, for the griddle
  • 1 pint fresh blueberries or 2 cups frozen blueberries, thawed
  • pure maple syrup, for serving

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, whisk the flour, baking powder and salt. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the milk, ricotta, sugar and vanilla. Add the dry ingredients and whisk until the batter is smooth.
  2. In a large bowl, using an electric mixer, beat the egg whites at medium speed until frothy. Beat at high speed until soft peaks form. Fold the egg whites into the batter until no streaks remain.
  3. Preheat the oven to 225Â°. Heat a griddle, then lightly butter it. For each pancake, ladle a scant 1/4 cup of the batter onto the griddle; be sure to leave enough space between the pancakes. Cook over moderately low heat until the bottoms are golden and the pancakes are just beginning to set, 1 to 2 minutes. Sprinkle each pancake with a few blueberries and press lightly. Flip the pancakes and cook until golden on the bottom and cooked through, about 1 minute longer. Transfer the pancakes to plates and keep them warm in the oven while you make the rest. Serve the pancakes with maple syrup.
